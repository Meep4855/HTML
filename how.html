How It Works

3D printing is based of a method called "photopolymerization" (This is when a liquid, is used to make a model that a computer gives it. It is constructed by layers and harden by a ultraviolet light) called "stereolithography" (a form of 3d printing technology that is used for creating models, prototypes, patterns, and production parts by layer.) You are gonna need a 3D model of what you are gonna print on either a USB or on a computer, that the 3D printer can read.

A few things to keep in mind before starting a new print

  1. Make sure the bed of the printer is level and secured. This can cause the bed to move and may cause mis prints
  2. Make sure you apply an adhesive. If you forget to apply the adhesive there is a chance you material might move around while printing.
  3. Also make sure you have enough of the materials you need to print.
